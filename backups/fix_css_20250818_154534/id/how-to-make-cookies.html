üìù Step-by-Step Cookie Making Guide

Step 1: Prepare Your Farm

Set up dedicated farming areas for cookie ingredients:

  • Plant wheat fields for flour production
  • Establish dairy farms for butter
  • Create sugar cane plantations
  • Set up chicken coops for eggs

Step 2: Gather Ingredients

Collect all necessary ingredients:

  • Flour: Harvest wheat and process in mill
  • Butter: Milk cows and churn butter
  • Sugar: Process sugar cane
  • Eggs: Collect from chicken coops
  • Chocolate: Process cocoa beans (optional)

Step 3: Mix Dough

Create the perfect cookie dough:

  • Cream butter and sugar until fluffy
  • Add eggs one at a time
  • Mix in flour and salt
  • Add chocolate chips or other mix-ins
  • Chill dough for 30 minutes

Step 4: Shape Cookies

Form the cookies:

  • Roll dough into small balls
  • Place on baking sheet with space between
  • Flatten slightly with fork or hand
  • Add decorative touches if desired

Step 5: Bake Cookies

Bake to perfection:

  • Preheat oven to correct temperature
  • Place cookies in center of oven
  • Bake for 10-12 minutes
  • Check for golden brown edges
  • Let cool on baking sheet

Step 6: Store and Sell

Package and sell your cookies:

  • Store in airtight containers
  • Package in attractive bundles
  • Set competitive prices
  • Advertise in community channels

Q: What's the best flour for making cookies?

A: All-purpose flour works best for most cookie recipes. Make sure to use fresh, high-quality flour for the best results.

Q: How long does it take to make cookies?

A: From ingredient gathering to finished cookies, expect 1-2 hours. With practice and proper setup, you can reduce this time significantly.

Q: Can I make cookies without an oven?

A: No, you need an oven to bake cookies. Make sure to build or purchase an oven before attempting cookie making.

Q: How much profit can I make from cookies?

A: Cookies typically sell for 2-3x the cost of ingredients. With efficient production, you can earn 300-600 coins per batch.

Q: What's the difference between regular and premium cookies?

A: Premium cookies use higher quality ingredients and sell for significantly more. Focus on quality over quantity for maximum profit.

Q: How do I prevent my cookies from being too hard?

A: Don't overbake them and store in airtight containers. Remove from oven when edges are golden but centers are still soft.
